What is Erasmus?

The Erasmus+ Programme is a European funding programme established in 1987 offering university students the possibility of studying or doing an internship abroad in another country for a period of at least 2 months and a maximum of 12 months per cycle of studies. Erasmus+ now offers the possibility to go way beyond the European borders as well.

Can you benefit?

Higher education students after completing a 1st year of studies can benefit from the Erasmus+ studies and Erasmus+ placement programmes. There is no age limitation.

More about Erasmus+

Money: Each student receives a grant which covers partly the costs of the stay abroad. Grants differ between sending and host countries. The Erasmus+ grant alone may not cover all expenses.

Courses: Before leaving, students sign a Learning Agreement to ensure the recognition of courses passed abroad.

Language: Students can take online language courses through Erasmus+ Online Linguistic Support.

Preparation: Contact your local ESN section or university for guidance. Planning 6 months ahead is recommended!
